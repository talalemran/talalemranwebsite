Whether you are looking to build a great website or start a new one from scratch, here are three factors for designing a standout site.

In this adapted article, you will learn the key elements for developing a distinctive online presence.

## 3 Factors for Designing a Standout Website

Our world today is highly technical; everything is on the internet. There is nothing you cannot buy, sell, trade, or research—nor any image you can't find or game you can't play—as long as you have a computer and an internet connection.

These days, there is a website for anything you can name. This means someone likely has a website very similar to yours. With increasing competition for page views, web design has become more technical. As the internet becomes more ubiquitous, good web design is more important than ever.

If you have a website, you want visitors to see it. Good web design is the only way to achieve that. It is also the only way to attract repeat visitors. There are several core essential elements of good web design that every designer must know how to use.

### 1. Website Content

Content is king. Internet search engines have become incredibly smart at analyzing website content these days. A standout web design revolves around what is on your site.

What keywords will people use to find you?

Once you identify your keywords, your site must feature plenty of content that highlights them.

You can use various free websites to check your keyword density and relevance.

Content is the first thing search engines look at, so you must make your mark there.

### 2. Website Links

Both search engines and visitors love links. You need internal links that connect to other pages on your site, as well as external links that connect to pages outside your domain.

The more quality links you have, the better—so use them freely throughout your site. Links also improve site navigation, which is a vital component of professional web design.

### 3. Graphics and Visuals

Graphics, bright colors, images, and playful fonts can make or break your website. Graphics will propagate through search engines based on how they appear and are tagged, so you want to leave a strong visual impression.

Good web design is about a pleasant flow of colors, images, graphics, and text. Do not clutter your pages, but keep them exciting and interesting to look at.

## Conclusion

Good web design is about knowing how to balance the core elements that attract both search engines and human viewers. Your pages should flow together, navigation should be effortless, and your site should be packed with valuable content.

The only way to increase traffic is through good web design that is updated regularly. This ensures new and repeat business for your site. The more visitors you have, the better—and great design is the only way to get there.
